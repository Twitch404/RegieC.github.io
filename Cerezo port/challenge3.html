Poverty-related Disparities in Access

Children from poorer households are less likely to attend school due to a lack of resources, low teacher salaries, and poor-quality facilities. In rural areas, schools often have overcrowded classrooms, outdated textbooks, and limited access to technology, making learning harder and limiting future opportunities.

Inadequate Facilities and Resources

Schools in low-income areas lack basic infrastructure like clean water, functional classrooms, and proper sanitation. Without modern technology or up-to-date textbooks, students miss out on a quality education necessary for success in the digital world.

Low Teacher Salaries and Support

Teachers in these areas are often underpaid and forced to use their own money for teaching materials. This, along with high turnover rates, disrupts learning and lowers the quality of education.

The Compounding Effect of Poverty

Poverty affects more than school attendance—it also impacts students' ability to succeed. Without support at home, many students struggle to complete assignments, creating a cycle of poverty that education struggles to break.
