Challenges

Education is a vital part of everyone’s future, but many students in the Philippines face serious obstacles.
There's no single "right way" to address these challenges, and some students are more deeply affected than others.

Factors like poverty, lack of resources, and family expectations all influence how children experience education.

The availability of schools, quality of teachers, and support systems can vary greatly between regions and communities.

These differences can make it harder for students to succeed and continue their education.

Being aware of these challenges can help us understand why some students struggle more than others.

By recognizing these issues, we can work towards better solutions and support for those in need.

Kulang ang funds

Kulang ang pondo. Many schools lack books, classrooms, and support. Teachers stretch thin, and students are left behind. Without enough funding, quality learning remains just a dream.

Kahirapan hadlang sa mga studyante

There aren’t enough teachers, but there are lots of students. Because of this, teachers get tired and can’t give enough help to each child. Learning becomes harder for everyone.

Unting guro, dami studyante

Poor kids often can’t go to school. They may need to work or stay home because they can’t afford school things. This makes it hard for them to have a better future.
