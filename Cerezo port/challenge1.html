The Big Barrier to Progress

Let’s be honest—education in the Philippines is full of potential.
But that potential often gets blocked by one big issue: kulang sa pondo.(lack of funding). This long-standing problem affects everyone in the system, from eager students to hardworking teachers.

Students Are the First to Suffer

Without enough funds, schools struggle to provide even the basics. Classrooms are packed. Textbooks are old. Computers? Often missing. Some students attend class in buildings with broken fans, damaged chairs, or no lights. It’s tough to focus when the learning space itself is falling apart.

Teachers Carry the Burden

Teachers try to make things work—but with low salaries and limited support, many are forced to spend their own money on materials like chalk, markers, or printed handouts. Some even take side jobs to survive. It’s an unfair load to carry when you're expected to inspire the future.

A Growing Divide

When schools lack funding, the gap between city and rural students grows. Some learn with fast Wi-Fi and cool rooms. Others sit in hot, crowded classrooms with no internet and broken chairs. It’s hard not to feel left out when your school feels forgotten. Every child deserves a fair chance—wherever they are.
